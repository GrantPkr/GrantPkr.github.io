Highlights of Interests

Outside of technical work, I value creative outlets, time outdoors, and activities that help me stay grounded and engaged.

Music Performance

One of my favorite ways to express creativity is through music. I grew up playing trombone and piano, and have played in many different ensembles over the years including jazz and classical settings. I also love electronic music, and enjoy setting time aside to attend concerts. Music has been one of the most consistent ways I have developed discipline, collaboration, and creative expression outside of technical work.

Skiing

Having been an undergraduate at CU, I have picked up an affinity for skiing. Getting out into the mountains is one of my favorite things to do. Skiing gives me a strong balance to academic and technical work, and it has become one of the ways I recharge, stay active, and appreciate the environment around Boulder.

AI Ethics and Responsible Automation

One of the interests that most strongly shapes how I think is AI ethics and the broader impact of automation. I am interested not only in building systems that work, but in understanding what happens when they are used in real settings where reliability, fairness, and accountability matter. This perspective has influenced how I think about software design, especially in projects involving intelligent behavior. I want my work to reflect responsibility and practical care, rather than treating those concerns as separate from engineering. For me, responsible automation means building systems that are useful, transparent, and dependable enough to support real people without creating unnecessary fragility or confusion.
