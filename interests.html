Highlights of Interests / Extracurricular

AI Ethics and Responsible Automation

One of the interests that most strongly shapes how I think is AI ethics and the broader impact of automation. I am interested not only in building systems that work, but in understanding what happens when they are integrated into real settings where reliability, fairness, and accountability matter. This perspective has influenced how I think about system design, especially in projects involving intelligent behavior. I want my work to reflect responsibility and practical care, rather than treating those concerns as something separate from engineering.

Collaboration Across Teams and Contexts

I also value collaboration as an important part of technical development. Through team-based work and sponsor-facing projects, I have seen how much communication shapes technical outcomes. Working across different expectations and styles has helped me learn that strong engineering is not only about writing code, but also about asking clear questions, adapting to constraints, and making sure the final result is understandable to others. That experience has helped me become more thoughtful about how projects move from ideas into usable systems.

Research and Technical Curiosity

Another area that distinguishes me is my interest in research-driven problem solving. I enjoy reading technical material, learning from papers, and figuring out how to move from theory into implementation. That process has taught me patience, persistence, and the importance of understanding not just what a method does, but why it works and where it breaks down. Research has made me better at investigating unknowns and communicating technical ideas in a way that is clearer and more grounded.
